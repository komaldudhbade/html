Wardha

Wardha was included in the empire of the Mauryas, Shungas, Satavahanas and Vakatakas.

Pravarapura, modern Pavnar, was once the capital of the Vakataka dynasty.


Vakatakas were contemporaries of the Imperial Guptas. Wardha has an adjacent city, Sevagram, and both were major centers for the Indian Independence Movement.

Pune

Pune also known as Poona, the official name until 1978) is the seventh most populous city in India and the second-largest city in the state of Maharashtra.

In the 18th century, the city was the seat of the Peshwas, the prime ministers of the Maratha Empire, and one of the most important political centres in the Indian subcontinent.


The city was previously also ruled by the Ahmadnagar Sultanate, the Mughals and the Adil Shahi dynasty. Historical landmarks include the Lal Mahal, the Kasba Ganapati temple and Shaniwar Wada.
